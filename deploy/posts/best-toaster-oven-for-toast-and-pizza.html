What to Look For

Convection Capability: Don't compromise on this. True convection (not just a circulation fan) is what separates adequate toaster ovens from good ones. It circulates hot air around your food, browning toast evenly and crisping pizza crusts properly. Budget models sometimes skip this feature—avoid them if both toast and pizza matter to you.

Interior Capacity and Pizza Size: Check the actual dimensions, not just cubic footage. A 0.8 cubic foot oven might not accommodate a standard 12-inch pizza. We recommend minimum 1.0 cubic feet if you regularly cook full pizzas. Measure your favorite pizza's dimensions and compare against the oven's interior—12 inches wide should be your baseline for comfort.

Temperature Precision and Range: Toast typically needs 350–425°F, while pizza performs best at 400–450°F. Make sure your oven reaches at least 450°F and maintains temperatures without wild swings. Models with digital temperature displays or preset functions tend to hold temperatures more consistently than those with simple dials. Better models include a built-in thermometer to prevent guessing games.

Interior Light and Visibility: This matters more than you'd think. Being able to see your toast browning and your pizza crust crisping without opening the door keeps oven temperature stable and prevents overdone spots. Models with bright LED interior lighting are worth the modest premium—they eliminate the "is it done yet?" guessing game that leads to disappointing results.
