What to Look For

Interior Space and Pizza Capacity

If pizza is important to you, measure before buying. Most toaster ovens fit a 12- to 14-inch pizza, but some can barely accommodate a 10-inch. The Breville and Cuisinart models we recommend both handle full-size 14-inch pizzas comfortably. Check the listed interior dimensions (length × width × height), not just the overall oven size. A claimed "large" oven sometimes feels cramped in practice.

Convection vs. Straight Heat

Convection heating (a fan that circulates hot air) is worth the small price premium. It reduces cooking time by 15–25%, prevents hot spots, and makes toast and pizza edges crisp while interiors stay soft. Every model we recommend includes convection. Avoid "convection-capable" wording—that often means an optional setting, not the standard mode.

Temperature Control and Presets

You want independent control of temperature and time, not just a dial labeled "light," "medium," "dark." The best models let you set exact temperatures (300°F to 500°F+) and run times. Preset functions for pizza, bagels, and toast are nice but should enhance, not replace, manual controls. Test this before buying—some digital controls are sluggish or confusing.

Build Quality and Durability

Look at the heating elements and interior construction. Nichrome or quartz elements last longer than cheaper coil designs. Stainless steel interiors resist staining better than painted surfaces. Read user reviews focused on durability, not just performance. A toaster oven that works great for six months but fails by year two is a bad investment at any price.
