Best Coffee Grinder for French Press: Our Top Picks for Coarse Grinds

French press brewing requires a specific grind size—too fine and you'll get muddy, over-extracted coffee; too coarse and it'll taste weak and hollow. The right grinder makes all the difference, and finding one that delivers consistent coarse grounds without breaking the bank can be surprisingly tricky.

What to Look For

Burr vs. Blade Grinders Always choose a burr grinder over a blade grinder for French press. Burr grinders use two abrasive surfaces (burrs) that crush beans consistently, producing uniform particle sizes. Blade grinders chop beans unevenly, resulting in a mix of fine powder and larger chunks. With French press, this inconsistency creates sediment and uneven extraction. Burr grinders cost more, but they're non-negotiable for proper French press brewing.

Grind Uniformity and Setting Range Look for a grinder with at least 15 settings, ideally with dedicated coarse options. French press requires a coarse grind—roughly the texture of sea salt. Grinders with more settings give you finer control, though more settings don't automatically mean better coffee. What matters is that the grinder produces particles within a narrow size range. Read reviews specifically mentioning French press to see if real users get consistent results.

Motor Power and Heat Generation A grinder's motor shouldn't produce excessive heat when grinding coarse (the motor runs longer with finer grinds). Overheating damages coffee's flavor compounds. Most burr grinders under $100 handle coarse grinding well. If you're grinding large quantities daily, a grinder with a more robust motor (like the Breville) stays cooler. For occasional use, heat isn't a practical concern.

Consistency and Durability Your grinder will be used hundreds of times per year. Look at user reviews mentioning long-term use (not just first impressions). Baratza's Encore and Comandante are known for lasting 5+ years with normal use. Cheaper blade grinders often fail within a year. Investing in a durable burr grinder saves money over time and ensures your morning coffee tastes good every day.

If you enjoy manual grinding and prioritize grind quality above all else, the Comandante C40 is worth the slight premium. Step up to the OXO Conical Burr if you want something slightly more refined without spending twice as much. Reserve the Breville Smart Grinder Pro for people who brew multiple methods and want precision control across all of them.

Avoid blade grinders entirely, regardless of price. A $25 burr grinder will make better French press than a $50 blade grinder every single time. The grinder is one area where spending a little more pays genuine dividends in cup quality and lasting value.
