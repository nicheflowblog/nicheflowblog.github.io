What to Look For in a Budget Air Fryer

Basket Size vs. Counter Space: Don't automatically buy the largest capacity. A 6-quart air fryer is pointless if it blocks your stove or leaves no work surface. Measure your counter and actual cooking needs first. A 4–5 quart model handles most dishes without excessive batching, and smaller models actually cook faster due to better air circulation in the basket.

Heating Element Reliability: This is where budget models fail. Look for units with proven track records (COSORI, Ninja, Instant Pot have established repair networks). Avoid no-name brands with no Amazon reviews deeper than 100 units sold. The heating element can't be easily replaced, so durability matters more than flashy features. Read reviews specifically mentioning longevity past one year.

Non-Stick Coating Quality: Cheaper basket coatings degrade quickly with daily use. COSORI and Ninja use more durable coatings than ultra-budget brands. If you plan to use this 3+ times weekly, spend the extra $15 for better coating durability. Hand-washing (not dishwasher) extends the life significantly. Check if the coating has survived 6+ months in verified customer reviews before buying.

Temperature Range and Control Method: You want at least 300–400°F capability for proper crisping. Digital controls with preset buttons are more reliable than touchscreens at this price point, though they're less convenient. Physical dials and buttons rarely fail, while budget touchscreens often malfunction within 18 months. If you cook delicate items regularly (fish, pastries), prioritize fine temperature control in 25°F increments.
