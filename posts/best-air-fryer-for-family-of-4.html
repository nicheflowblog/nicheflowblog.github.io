What to Look For When Choosing an Air Fryer for Four People

Capacity matters more than you think. A 4-quart basket technically holds food for four, but realistically, you'll need 5+ quarts to cook a complete family meal without crowding. Overcrowded baskets create uneven cooking and soggy spots. The Cosori Pro Gen 2 and Instant Pot Vortex Plus both offer this capacity at reasonable prices. If space is truly limited, accept that you'll cook in two shifts.

Temperature accuracy determines cooking consistency. Air fryers vary dramatically in how they maintain set temperatures. The Cosori models hold stable temperatures throughout the cooking cycle, while some budget options fluctuate, leading to inconsistent results. This matters more than any other specification when feeding four hungry people who expect the same quality meal every time.

Basket design affects usable space more than capacity numbers suggest. A square basket maximizes cooking area compared to cylindrical designs. Compare the actual dimensions, not just quart measurements. Shallow, wide baskets let you arrange food properly without stacking, which improves texture and cooking speed.

Cleaning effort is a real-world factor you'll live with daily. Non-stick coatings vary significantly. Stainless steel interiors resist damage but occasionally show minor sticking. Dishwasher-safe baskets save genuine time. Test whether the manufacturer's recommended hand-washing actually makes sense—some "dishwasher safe" baskets have components that wash better by hand.

The square design means you'll rarely need to cook in batches for a typical family meal.

If you frequently cook elaborate meals with multiple components or have teenagers with large appetites, upgrade to the Cosori Max XL. The extra $50 investment eliminates cooking in shifts entirely and provides insurance against the appliance becoming too small as your family grows.

Avoid the temptation to buy oversized commercial models unless you genuinely cook for more than four people regularly. They consume excess counter space, take longer to preheat, and provide no benefit if your actual cooking volume is modest.
